Bearer of a Project Management Professional certificate (PMP), a credential offered by the Project Management Institute (PMI) in the United States, with more than nineteen years of work experience in related positions.

Doing comprehensive research, graduated from George Washington University’s political management program and learned the most important aspects of U.S campaigns. This program is designed for those who are interested in Americans politics and campaigns and offers substantial courses such as Campaign Strategy, Communication Strategy, Fundraising, Targeting and Voter Files, Digital Media, Data Analysis, etc.

During term of study, carried out lots of in-depth research for different courses such as a “Campaign Digital Strategy Plan for a Hypothetical U.S 2016 Presidential Election” (in the case of Joe Biden), “Strategy Behind the Hillary Clinton 2016 Presidential Campaign” (based on specific poll results), “Campaign Communication Plan for Hillary Clinton”, “Campaign Strategy Plan for Hypothetical Candidate in District 33 California U.S Congressional Election”, “Targeting Plan for Patrick Murphy in 2016 Senate Election in Florida”, “Strategies Behind Bill Clinton’s Re-Election Bid in 1996”, “Rise of an Outsider in the U.S Presidential Election (Case Study: Donald Trump)”, etc.

In 2011, obtained MBA from Carleton University of Canada and chose corporate social responsibility (CSR) as the topic of the thesis and conducted research about the “Application of CSR in Global Companies (Case Study: BP). Thesis was graded “A.”

Currently, studying in the Data Analytics program at George Washington University and believe that the area of data analytics and the ability to interpret and visualize numbers is crucial for anyone who is working on campaign-related, as well as business-related activities.

In June 2013, owing to the passion for the analytical approach, with some friends conducted the first-ever public opinion tracking poll for Iran’s presidential election (http://www.ipos.me/en/). Also conducted a number of other polls gauging Iranian opinion on a variety of social-political issues (such as President Rohani’s job approval rating), the results of which have all been published on the iPOS website.
